The o+a palette library

The palette library brings together the colour palettes of every work in our collection. You can browse by painting, move through individual swatches, or filter by colour to see how certain colours are used across centuries of image-making.

Each colour is paired with the closest historical name we can find, drawn from pigment catalogues, dye records, and naturalist guides — sources like Werner's Nomenclature of Colours (1821), the Winsor & Newton pigment archive, and earlier texts like Cennini's Il Libro dell'Arte. These names are matched computationally to the nearest documented colour, then listed alongside the painting they come from.

This resource is less a fixed system than a way of seeing. Colours that feel contemporary often have long histories, and combinations that seem intuitive tend to repeat. The library is open and ongoing — we add to it as the collection grows, and the full dataset is freely available from the export drawer.
